5 Best Practices for ETL Development with AWS Glue

As organizations increasingly rely on data to drive their business decisions, the demand for efficient and reliable ETL processes has never been greater. AWS Glue is a powerful and fully-managed ETL service that makes it easy to move and transform data between data stores. In this article, we will share 5 best practices for ETL development with AWS Glue to help you get the most out of this service.

1. Design for scalability

One of the main benefits of using AWS Glue is the ability to scale your ETL processes to meet the needs of your organization. When designing your ETL pipelines, it's important to consider how they will scale as the volume and complexity of your data grows. This might involve designing your pipelines with parallelism in mind, using partitioning to divide large datasets into smaller chunks, or leveraging the power of AWS Glue's data processing capabilities to handle large volumes of data.
